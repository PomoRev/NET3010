ALL ABOUT SEMANTIC HTML
definition of semantic - semiotics is the study of signs in literature. Those things that point to something else as a kind of signpost in the road. Signs are much simpler than symbols as they state plainly what they are pointing at where symbols remain polyvalent even when collapsed into a meaning. A sign in this sense is like a traffic sign, only to be interpretted in a singular way. When the sign says stop, you better listen.

Anatomy of the Web Page

The semantic layout is all about organizing our webpage in such a way as the labels provide a signpost guide to the content. These signs allow us to easily and logically target sections of our website for formatting and interaction. These labes consist of id tags and class tags. Id tags are always singular, as in you have one #header, and class tags are often plural as in you can have many .article blocks. Semantic layout is achieved only when your tags tell the story of how your document is structured.
