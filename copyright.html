How to Cite Images

There are several ways to safely use creative content as an academic. Because you are using the work for academic purposes a simple comment in the HTML file, before or after the image, will be sufficient. This attribution must contain a link to the original website and information about the creator(s) of the image.

If your content is meant to exist on the web then you are better off following the rule of whatever licensing the content was released under. If no such relase was made you are advised to find content that is released as copyright violations are serious for academics and ignoring the rules can cost you your degree - even a successfully completed degree!

Finally note that remixing or modifying images can sometimes be prohibited by the licensing agreement. Read the licensing information carefully. Images that specify free for commercial use and no attribution required are usable without attribution.

The same rules apply to video, audio, and other content that does not explicitely belong to you.
